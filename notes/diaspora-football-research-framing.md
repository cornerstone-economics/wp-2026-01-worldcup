## Two-piece strategy

This project produces two outputs from the same underlying mechanism (diaspora football economics):

1. **Dick Draayer article** — journalism piece on the Aruba women's 6-1 win over Curaçao (April 13, 2026). Publish while the result is still live news. Hook + reveal + one good Verhoeve quote. Keep the working paper framework almost entirely out of it. Companion to the existing Curaçao men's World Cup piece at `09-homo-agapicus/Dick-Draayer.md`.

2. **Working paper** — formalizes the diaspora mechanism and shows it predicts different outcomes across gender lines for the same two islands. The Aruba women's case is the falsification test. Hold 4-8 weeks after the Draayer piece, timed to land as the men's World Cup narrative heats up.

The combination insulates against any "taking Curaçao down" reading. Same analyst, same framework, opposite conclusions depending on which sport.

## Core analytical puzzle

Why does Aruba execute the diaspora strategy successfully in women's football but not in men's, while Curaçao does it successfully in men's but not in women's?

The answer lies in pipeline size and federation execution. Men's football has a vast Dutch-Antillean diaspora pool where passive selection works (favoring Curaçao as historical successor to Netherlands Antilles). Women's football has a thinner pool where targeted recruitment moves the needle (favoring Aruba's aggressive strategy under van der Laan).
